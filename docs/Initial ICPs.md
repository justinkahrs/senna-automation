1. CNC / Machine Shops (Primary)

ICP Profile

Industry: CNC machining / precision manufacturing
Size: 5–25 employees
Revenue: ~$1M–$10M
Location: Local or regional (start West Michigan)
Owner: Hands-on, often involved in quoting

⸻

Operational Reality
• Work comes in via:
• Email (mostly)
• Attachments (STEP files, drawings, PDFs)
• Quotes are:
• Manually reviewed
• Manually calculated
• Sent back via email
• Follow-ups are inconsistent or nonexistent

⸻

Core Pain

“We get a lot of quote requests, but responding takes time and things slip through.”

Breakdown:
• Inbox overload
• Time spent reviewing specs
• Slow response → lost jobs
• No structured pipeline

⸻

Trigger Moments
• Missing jobs due to slow replies
• Hiring admin help just to manage email
• Growth → chaos in quoting

⸻

Buying Mindset
• Logical, efficiency-driven
• Doesn’t care about “AI”
• Cares about:
• Speed
• Throughput
• Not losing work

⸻

Your Offer (mapped)

“Turn incoming RFQ emails into structured quotes + automatic follow-ups”

2. Construction / Contractors

ICP Profile

Industry: General contractors or subcontractors (electrical, HVAC, plumbing)
Size: 5–50 employees
Revenue: ~$2M–$20M
Owner: Operator + project manager hybrid

⸻

Operational Reality
• Leads come from:
• Email
• Phone
• Website forms
• Estimates involve:
• Back-and-forth communication
• Missing info
• Jobs are tracked:
• In email threads
• Or scattered tools

⸻

Core Pain

“We’re constantly juggling estimates, jobs, and communication, and things fall through.”

Breakdown:
• Messy estimate intake
• Missed follow-ups
• Poor job visibility
• Time wasted chasing info

⸻

Trigger Moments
• Losing bids due to slow response
• Jobs getting delayed from miscommunication
• Too many active projects → chaos

⸻

Buying Mindset
• Results-driven, impatient
• Less polished than manufacturing
• Will buy if it clearly saves time or wins jobs

⸻

Your Offer (mapped)

“Capture estimate requests, organize them, and automate follow-ups so you don’t lose jobs”

3. Logistics / Freight

ICP Profile

Industry: Freight brokers / small logistics companies
Size: 3–30 employees
Revenue: ~$1M–$15M
Role: Dispatcher / broker juggling communication

⸻

Operational Reality
• Everything runs through:
• Email
• Phone
• Tasks include:
• Coordinating loads
• Updating statuses
• Responding to inquiries
• Very time-sensitive

⸻

Core Pain

“We’re buried in emails and constant communication just to keep things moving.”

Breakdown:
• Inbox chaos
• Manual updates
• Repetitive communication
• High cognitive load

⸻

Trigger Moments
• Scaling load volume → overwhelmed
• Missing updates → unhappy clients
• Hiring more dispatchers

⸻

Buying Mindset
• Speed-focused
• ROI is obvious if time is saved
• Less patient for long implementations

⸻

Your Offer (mapped)

“Turn incoming emails into structured load workflows + automate updates and responses”
