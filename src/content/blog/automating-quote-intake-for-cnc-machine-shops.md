For many CNC machine shops, new business starts in the inbox.

Requests for quotes arrive throughout the day, often with a mix of attachments like STEP files, PDFs, and drawings. Each request needs to be reviewed, understood, and quoted quickly. But as volume increases, that process becomes difficult to manage.

We built a system that automatically ingests incoming RFQs, extracts key job details, and organizes them into a structured pipeline so teams can focus on quoting instead of sorting through emails.

## The challenge: RFQs scattered across email

Most shops rely heavily on email for new work. That creates several problems:

- Requests are easy to miss
- Important details are buried in attachments
- No consistent way to prioritize jobs
- Follow-ups are inconsistent

As the business grows, these issues compound and lead to missed opportunities.

## The solution: Structured quote intake

The system monitors incoming emails and processes each RFQ automatically.

It extracts information such as:

- Part quantities
- Materials
- Tolerances
- Delivery timelines

Each request is then scored and routed into a structured pipeline, giving the team a clear view of what to quote next.

## The outcome: Faster response, more wins

With a structured intake system, the shop can respond to high-value opportunities faster and with more consistency.

That leads to:

- Reduced response times
- Better prioritization
- Increased quote volume without additional admin work

Instead of reacting to a cluttered inbox, the team operates from a clean, prioritized queue of real opportunities.
