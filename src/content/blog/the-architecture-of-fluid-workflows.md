Fluidity in workflow design is about reducing the number of moving parts. When we design systems with fewer dependencies, they are inherently more robust.

## Minimalist Principles

A minimalist workflow is like a clean codebase. It's easy to understand, easy to debug, and most importantly, easy to change.

  1. **Simplicity over Complexity**: Always choose the simplest tool for the job.
  2. **Decoupling**: Ensure that a failure in one node doesn't bring down the whole system.
  3. **Observability**: If something fails, you should know exactly why.

### Designing for Failure

We don't build systems that never fail. We build systems that fail gracefully.
